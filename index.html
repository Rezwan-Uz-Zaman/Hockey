What is the duration of a standard ice hockey game?

A standard ice hockey game consists of three periods, each lasting 20 minutes, with intermissions in between.

What is the purpose of the blue lines on a hockey rink?

The blue lines divide the rink into three zones: defensive, neutral, and offensive. They are also used to determine offsides.

What is a power play in hockey?

A power play happens when one team has a numerical advantage on the ice due to one or more opponents serving penalties.

What is the Stanley Cup?

The Stanley Cup is the championship trophy awarded annually to the NHL playoff winner.

What is the "penalty box" in hockey?

The penalty box is an area where players serve time for infractions, leaving their team short-handed during the penalty duration.
